Biak Island: Papua’s Gateway to Coral Gardens & Living Heritage

Welcome to Biak (Kepulauan Biak), where clear water, colorful coral, and warm island culture meet poignant WWII history. Use this page to discover must-do experiences and plan a respectful, memorable trip.

Quick Facts

  • Location: Cenderawasih Bay, Papua (Indonesia)
  • Best time: Generally dry & calm seas: May–October
  • Known for: Snorkeling, birdlife, caves, WWII relics

Experiences & Photo Highlights

Top 5 Experiences

  1. Snorkel & dive among vibrant hard corals and reef fish along Biak’s fringing reefs.
  2. WWII heritage at caves and coastal sites that tell the island’s 1944 campaign story.
  3. Birding at dawn: watch for parrots, hornbills, and endemics in coastal forest.
  4. Blue caves & lagoons with striking limestone pools perfect for a cool dip.
  5. Markets & culture—taste smoked fish, sago, and meet friendly Biak islanders.

Plan Your Visit

Getting there: Biak’s Frans Kaisiepo Airport (BIK) connects via major Indonesian hubs.

Respect & safety: Dress modestly in villages, ask before photographing people, and follow local guidance at heritage sites.

What to pack:

  • Reef-safe sunscreen, hat, and lightweight long sleeves
  • Mask/snorkel or dive log if you plan to explore the reefs
  • Sturdy sandals for caves/limestone areas

Learn more: Read Biak Island on Wikipedia for background and history.

Travel kindly. Coral and cultural heritage are fragile—please tread lightly and support local guides and small businesses.
